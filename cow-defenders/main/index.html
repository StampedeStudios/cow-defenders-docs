Story Lineüìñ

Earth in 2053, humans have been living without electricity for 12 years following a worldwide Black Out called “The Big Black Out”. The cause of this catastrophe is attributed to unexpected solar activity. Humans have adapted, living through agriculture in close contact with nature in a period resembling a slightly more advanced 1900’s. They are no longer masters of the world; at the onset of the Big Black Out, there was complete anarchy, but after 12 years, a government based on a religion condemning the past and electricity had consolidated. The sun is revered by this new religion for having rid humanity of electricity, returning it to nature. Anything from the past not used for survival is condemned. People have distanced themselves from cities, considered places where humans should never set foot again to atone for their sins. The established government is ruthless, ready to execute anyone who disrespects the rules and the religion. The protagonist is Sam, a gruff mountain man in his fifties, who has distanced himself from both cities and people. He dislikes the new world and yearns for the world of the past.

Sam was a Machine Learning Engineer with a family when the Big Black Out occurred. They managed to survive for the first few years, but his son and wife fell ill, and without technology, he couldn’t save them. After the death of his family, he distanced himself from everything, condemning the present world.

He lives on a farm far from everyone and everything, sustains himself through agriculture, and has only one hobby‚Äîattempting to make electricity work. One day, he manages to make his computer work and is contacted by an alien artificial intelligence that helps him restore electricity, revealing that the malfunction is not due to natural causes but a virus.

The AI explains to Sam that this is part of military tactics by another alien species ready to take over Earth by eliminating the technologies of their adversaries. It teaches Sam how to cure the world of this virus to prompt humanity to react to the invasion. However, Sam’s plans are different; upon discovering the possibility of an invasion, he activates to defend everything dear to him‚Äîthe memories of the past world.

Sam doesn’t care about saving the world, only the memory of the past. Despite the AI’s urging, after several attempts to convince Sam to rejoin humanity, he reprograms the AI to help him only in his objective.

In just one week, he develops never-before-seen technologies to protect himself from the aliens and begins the wait for the invasion.

Economyüí∏

1 Laboratory

It allows you to unlock new upgrades from the menu using persisten-currency. The upgrades are in a tree structure. There are two kind of upgrades. The first kind is the most common one, the upgrade is available from the start of the level (more damage, faster rate of fire, cheaper costs, slow down enemy etc‚Ä¶). The second kind of upgrade can be obtained only after you have unlocked an entire branch of the tree upgrades. This upgrade is not available from the start of the level, you have to power up a turret to the max before being able to use it. This adds a unique and strong feature to a turret that can be further upgraded. Potential name of this unique upgrades is:

Power Up. LORE: All the technologies of the farm and the laboratory are created by an AI who contacted you before the break out.

2 Persistent Currency

You get persistent currency at the end of the level based on your performance (damage received to your structure).
